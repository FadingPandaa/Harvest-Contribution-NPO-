Harvest Contribution delivers a wide range of services related to the fight against food insecurity by improving systems and structures related to safe, sustainable, and culturally appropriate food in disadvantaged communities. The major services they have are as follows:


    1. Community Garden Development:
  • Establishing: They build community gardens in vacant lots, schools, and all other underutilized spaces. They would be easily accessible to the people in the locality.
  • Support and Resources: The organization provides seeds, tools, and other materials to begin and to maintain the community gardens.


    • 2. Nutritional Education:
  • Workshops and Training: Harvest Contribution organizes workshops on how to garden, practice sustainable agriculture, and learn how to eat healthily. These educational classes outline planting, harvesting, and then the preparation of nutritious meals.
  • Cooking Classes: Cooking classes are conducted that teach how to prepare healthy meals at an affordable cost using fresh produce from the gardens.


    • 3. Volunteer and Community Engagement:
  • Volunteer Coordination: They facilitate opportunities for volunteers to contribute their time and skills to garden projects, educational programs, and community events.
  • Community Events: Harvest Contribution holds events for the community through harvest festivals and educational fairs that bring people together to celebrate the fruit of their labor.


    • 4. Local Partnerships and Collaboration:
  • Partnership Development: The organization works with local businesses and schools, as well as other non-profit organizations, to forge stronger community relations and expand the programs' reach.
  • Resource Sharing: They are partnered to share additional produce with food banks, shelters, and others that get produce to people in need.


    • 5. Sustainability and Environmental Preservation:
  • Composting and Recycling: They promote composting and other sustainability practices in the gardens to reduce landfill waste and increase soil quality.
  • Water Conservation: It involves the application of water-saving methods and educates communities on sustainable irrigation techniques.
